NRI investment in Mangalore — the complete 2026 guide

If you're an NRI considering a property purchase in Mangalore, this guide covers the full end-to-end process: eligibility, banking, loans, documents, registration, taxes, and repatriation — with actionable specifics rather than generic advice.

"Everything an NRI needs to know — in one place."

1. Are you eligible?

Under FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act) regulations, the following categories can buy residential and commercial property in India without RBI approval:

  • NRI — Non-Resident Indian with a valid Indian passport
  • OCI — Overseas Citizen of India card-holder
  • PIO — Person of Indian Origin (now subsumed into OCI)

Foreign nationals who are not NRI/OCI cannot buy property in India without specific RBI permission. Agricultural land, plantation property and farmhouses are not permitted for NRI/OCI buyers — even residential apartments built on such land have restrictions.

2. The banking setup

You'll need one of three NRI-specific bank accounts. Most buyers use a combination:

NRE Account (Non-Resident External)

Your foreign earnings parked in India. Fully repatriable (including interest). Use this for the bulk of your purchase payment. Interest is tax-free in India.

NRO Account (Non-Resident Ordinary)

Your Indian earnings — rental income, dividends, pension. Limited repatriation: up to USD 1 million per financial year after tax. Interest is taxable.

FCNR Account (Foreign Currency Non-Resident)

Foreign currency deposits (USD, GBP, EUR etc.) — protects against rupee depreciation. Can be converted to NRE for payment if needed.

Practical tip: open both NRE and NRO with the same bank (preferably one with good Mangalore branch coverage — SBI, ICICI, HDFC, Canara are all strong). This makes transfers and coordination simpler.

3. The documents checklist

  • Valid passport + visa (or OCI card)
  • PAN card (mandatory — apply via Form 49AA if you don't have one)
  • Proof of overseas address (utility bill, bank statement)
  • Proof of Indian address, if any
  • Latest 6-month NRE/NRO/FCNR account statement
  • Employment proof / income proof (for loans)
  • Power of Attorney (if purchasing via proxy)
  • KYC (photograph, signature)

4. Financing — NRI home loans

Most Indian banks (ICICI, HDFC, SBI, Axis, LIC Housing Finance) offer NRI home loans. Key parameters:

  • Loan-to-Value: up to 80% of property value
  • Tenure: up to 20 years (must be repaid before age 60 typically)
  • Interest rate: 8.75-9.75% as of 2026 (floating)
  • EMI payment: must be from NRE/NRO accounts only
  • Processing fee: 0.25-1% of loan amount

Tip: most banks will also require a local guarantor (often a family member). Our NRI desk coordinates this end-to-end.

5. Power of Attorney — the game-changer for remote buyers

A Power of Attorney (PoA) allows a trusted person in India to sign documents on your behalf. Most NRIs issue a "Specific PoA" (for the single transaction) to a parent or sibling.

How to execute it

  1. Draft the PoA (we provide a standard template).
  2. Notarise it at your country's Indian consulate / embassy.
  3. Courier the original to India.
  4. Within 3 months of arrival in India, the PoA must be adjudicated by the Sub-Registrar (stamp duty paid, typically ₹1,000-2,000).

Once adjudicated, your family member can sign the sale deed, register the property, and collect keys — all without you flying down.

6. Registration & stamp duty

In Karnataka, registration is mandatory and involves:

  • Stamp duty: 5% of the higher of consideration or guidance value
  • Registration fee: 1% additional
  • Cess: 10% of stamp duty

Payments are made online via Kaveri Online Services. The Sub-Registrar's office (Mangalore, Ullal, etc.) then registers the deed. With PoA, your family member attends — you don't need to fly.

7. Taxes — what you pay

During purchase

TDS at 1% of consideration value if property is above ₹50 lakhs. The builder deducts this and deposits with the income tax department against your PAN.

During ownership

Property tax (municipal) and any rental income tax (30% slab + surcharge if applicable). Rental income can be offset against municipal tax, standard 30% deduction, and home loan interest.

On sale

Long-term capital gains (holding >2 years) — 20% with indexation. Short-term (<2 years) — taxed at your slab rate.

Section 54 exemption: if you reinvest the capital gain into another residential property within 2 years (or construct within 3 years), the tax is fully exempt.

8. Repatriating sale proceeds

Good news: the rules are generous. You can repatriate:

  • Full sale proceeds of up to 2 residential properties per lifetime if originally bought from NRE funds
  • Up to USD 1 million per financial year for other properties
  • Funds must go through NRE/NRO and require CA certificate (Form 15CA/15CB)

9. The Mangalore-specific advantages

Why Mangalore specifically, versus Bangalore or other metros?

  • Family anchor. Most Mangalore NRIs have extended family here — property fits naturally.
  • Lower entry. Premium 3 BHK in Mangalore: ₹1-1.5 cr versus ₹3-4 cr in comparable Bangalore areas.
  • Better yields. 3-4% rental yields versus 2-2.5% in metros.
  • Predictable supply. Limited new land = naturally capped supply = steady appreciation.
  • Emotional return. A place to come back to — something a tier-1 metro investment can't offer.

10. The trap to avoid

Unscrupulous middlemen. We've seen NRI buyers pay 15-25% over fair value because they relied on an "agent" quoting local prices. Always:

  • Buy from RERA-registered builders (non-negotiable)
  • Verify RERA registration on rera.karnataka.gov.in
  • Get independent legal title verification before booking
  • Pay a booking amount only via banking channels (never cash)
  • Insist on a price breakdown — base price, GST, amenities, registration costs
Buying property from overseas is easier than it has ever been. The tools, laws, and banking systems are all well-established. What matters most is who you buy from.
